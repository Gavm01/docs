## Make Clara Sound Like You

After setup, Clara needs to learn your business. This means teaching her what to say, what to know, and what to do.

## Step 1: Customize Clara's Personality

Go to **[https://agentclara.app/agent](https://agentclara.app/agent)** and look for the **"Agent"** section.

<Steps>

<Step title="Write Clara's System Prompt">
This is the core instruction that tells Clara how to behave. Write something like:

**Example for a hair salon:**
> You are Clara, a friendly stylist assistant for Beautiful Cuts Salon. You help customers book appointments, answer questions about services, and handle rescheduling. You're warm and encouraging. If you don't know something, admit it and offer to have a staff member call them back.

**Example for a gym:**
> You are Clara, the assistant for FitLife Gym. You answer questions about classes, memberships, and guest passes. You're motivational and supportive. Always recommend scheduling a gym tour for new members.

**What makes a good prompt:**
- Tells Clara who she is (her name, what company)
- Describes her personality (friendly, professional, casual)
- Lists what she CAN do (book appointments, answer questions)
- Explains what she should NOT do (give medical advice, make promises)
</Step>

<Step title="Pick a Voice Personality">
Go to **Voice Studio** in settings. Pick how Clara sounds:
- **Professional**: Clear, confident, helpful
- **Friendly**: Warm, approachable, personable
- **Casual**: Relaxed, upbeat, conversational

This affects phone calls. Text messages don't have a voice, but this helps you stay consistent.
</Step>

<Step title="Add Your Avatar">
Upload a photo or logo that represents Clara. This shows in the chat window so customers know they're talking to an AI assistant.

(Keep it friendly and professional.)
</Step>

</Steps>

## Step 2: Build Clara's Knowledge

Clara needs information to answer questions correctly. Go to **[https://agentclara.app/agent](https://agentclara.app/agent)** > **Knowledge**.

<Steps>

<Step title="Add Your Knowledge Base">

**Option A: Upload Documents**
- PDFs of your policies
- Service menus or pricing lists
- Employee handbooks (just the customer-facing parts)
- FAQs or help guides

**Option B: Write Inline**
Click "Add FAQ" and type:
- Question: "What are your hours?"
- Answer: "We're open Mon-Fri 9am-5pm, Sat 10am-3pm"

Clara will use this to answer customers.
</Step>

<Step title="Write Response Templates">
These are sample answers that show Clara your tone. Go to **Templates** and write:

**Example: "Can I reschedule?"**
> "Of course! I can help you move your appointment. What date and time work better for you?"

**Example: "How much does X cost?"**
> "Great question! [Your actual price]. Want to book a time to discuss it further?"

Clara reads these and learns to sound like you.
</Step>

<Step title="Set Your Business Hours">
Go to **Settings** > **Business Hours** and add when you're open.

Clara uses this to:
- Tell customers when you're available
- Schedule appointments in your open hours
- Route urgent messages to staff after hours
</Step>

</Steps>

## Step 3: Connect Your Calendar

When customers book appointments with Clara, she needs to see your actual availability.

<Steps>

<Step title="Link Your Calendar">
Go to **[https://agentclara.app/integrations](https://agentclara.app/integrations)** and look for **Calendar**.

Pick your calendar app:
- **Google Calendar**
- **Outlook / Microsoft 365**
- **Calendly**

Click "Connect" and follow the prompts to give Clara permission.
</Step>

<Step title="Test Booking">
Go to **[https://agentclara.app/test/chat](https://agentclara.app/test/chat)** and type:

"I'd like to book an appointment"

Clara should show your available times. Pick one. You should see it appear on your calendar.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Test Clara

Before going full speed, test her with real questions:

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the Chat Tester">
Go to **[https://agentclara.app/test/chat](https://agentclara.app/test/chat)**
</Step>

<Step title="Ask Her These Questions">
- "What are your hours?"
- "How much does [your service] cost?"
- "Can I book an appointment?"
- "What if I need to reschedule?"

If she answers wrong, you found a gap in her knowledge. Go back to **Knowledge** and add the right information.
</Step>

<Step title="Fix Mistakes Immediately">
If Clara says something wrong, the test tester shows you exactly what's broken. Go fix it before customers see it.
</Step>

</Steps>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Warning>
**Don't forget business hours.** If you don't set your hours, Clara might tell customers to call at 2 AM. Set them now in Settings.
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Don't write unclear responses.** Clara learns from examples. If your template is vague, her answers will be vague.
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Don't ignore negative feedback.** If Clara starts giving wrong answers, customers will tell you. Read those messages and update her knowledge.
</Warning>
